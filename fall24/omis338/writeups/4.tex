\noindent Considering the production line achieving an output rate of 120 per hour with the following proposed design:
\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{ ./1.png }
\end{figure}
\noindent To find the cycle time (CT), we can use the formula:
$$ CT = A/R$$
Where, 
$$ A = 60^2 = 3600\text{ (sec)}$$
$$ R = 120 $$
This gives us:
$$ CT = \dfrac{3600}{120} = 30  \ seconds$$
\bigbreak \noindent
Given the following list of stations and their assigned tasks, we can compute the total amount of time (in seconds) it takes to complete their tasks, along with each stations idle time (in seconds), using the formula:
$$ (N \cdot CT) - \sum{t}$$

\noindent To find the assembly line efficiency. We use the formula:
$$ \frac{\small{\sum}{t}}{N \cdot CT}$$
Which gives us:
$$ \dfrac{150}{5 \cdot 30} = 1$$
